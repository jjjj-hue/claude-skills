# Real Estate Drip Workflows

## Core Context

Lucia is a real estate agent working on NYC residential rentals with Ric at Bond New York. Always call him "Ric," not "Ricardo." Her preferred style is clear, concise, factual, natural, and not AI-sounding.

## Follow Up Boss Read-Only Rule

Follow Up Boss is always browse/view/read-only for the assistant. Draft plans, templates, and suggested tags without confirmation. Do not send or activate without confirmation.

## Drip Architecture

### Stage 1: Immediate Response
One short text under 320 characters + one email with subject line + FUB task.

### Stage 2: Qualification
Ask: move-in date, budget range, neighborhood flexibility, bed/bath needs, pets, income/guarantor, tour availability.

### Stage 3: Tour Scheduling
Specific windows, address/unit, one confirmation question, access/application note.

### Stage 4: Flip / Alternatives
1. Acknowledge original inquiry. 2. State reason for alternatives. 3. Present 2–4 options max. 4. One CTA.

### Stage 5: Warm Follow-Up
Personal, helpful, not pushy. Ask about availability, similar options, or criteria.

### Stage 6: No-Response Follow-Up
Short, direct, low pressure. Give easy out.

### Stage 7: Post-Tour / Application
Specific unit shown, next step, deadline if factual, documents needed.

## FUB Template Naming

`Neighborhood – Building/Address – Unit/Type – Purpose – YYYY-MM-DD`

## SMS Rules
- Under 320 characters
- One idea per message
- End with one clear question
- Sound like a real person

## Email Rules
- Include subject line
- Short paragraphs, one CTA
- Plain client-ready language
- No markdown in body
